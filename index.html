NASA

  • 1. Name of NASA's Owner: NASA is a U.S. government agency, and there is no specific individual identified as the owner of the agency. It operates under the authority of the U.S. government.

  • 2. NASA's Establishment Year: NASA was established on July 29, 1958, under the National Aeronautics and Space Act signed by President Dwight D. Eisenhower.

  • 3. First Rocket Launch: The first rocket launch by NASA took place on January 24, 1959. The rocket launched was the Jupiter C Explorer.

  • 4. Number of Rockets Launched So Far: The number of rockets launched by NASA is constantly being updated. It is recommended to refer to reliable sources for the latest information on the number of rockets launched.

  • 5. NASA's Budget: NASA's budget varies from year to year and is subject to changes in government funding. In recent years, NASA's budget has ranged from $20 to $25 billion annually.

  • 6. Countries Supporting NASA: NASA is an American space agency, but it collaborates with many other countries in the field of space exploration. Countries that support and work with NASA include various European countries such as the United Kingdom, Germany, France, Italy, as well as Japan, Russia, Canada, and many others.
